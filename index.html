Quevedo

Biography and Rise to Fame

Pedro Domínguez Quevedo, born in Madrid in 2001 and raised in Gran Canaria since age four, quickly established himself as a key figure in global urban music. His breakthrough came in the summer of 2022 with the "Music Sessions #52" alongside Bizarrap. This success quickly sent him to number 1 on Spotify's 'Top 50: Global' chart, making him the first Spanish artist to do so and marking a platform milestone. This achievement confirmed that Quevedo is the Spanish artist who has spent the most time leading the Global Spotify chart.

Debut Album and Consolidation (2023)

On January 20, 2023, Quevedo released his first full-length album, 'Donde Quiero Estar' (Where I Want to Be). Described as an intimate introduction to the world with the goal of becoming "an album for everyone and forever," the work included 12 solo tracks and 4 collaborations, notably the single "Playa Del Inglés" with his idol Myke Towers. Throughout 2023, Quevedo maintained his lead, gained global recognition with the hit "Columbia," was awarded a Latin Grammy, and completed a tour of over 40 concerts. This growth phase was symbolically closed on February 1, 2024, with the song "LA ÚLTIMA" (The Last One), fulfilling the phrase "2023 to crown, 2024 to disappear."

The Second Album and Redefinition (2024)

His highly anticipated second studio album, "Buenas Noches" (Good Nights) (2024), reflects a new life perspective and a personal commitment after "stepping away from the noise." With this work, Quevedo aims to redefine the international urban music scene in Spanish. The album, which combines impactful rhythms and his unmistakable style, promises to be one of the year's top releases. Furthermore, it demonstrates his artistic versatility by featuring a distinguished list of collaborators, including Aitana, De La Ghetto, Pitbull, Rels B, and Yung Beef, among others.

International Collaborations and Leadership

In addition to his album successes, Quevedo has positioned himself internationally through an extensive list of collaborations. He has worked with renowned artists such as Ed Sheeran ('2 Step'), Karol G ('Pero Tú'), Duki ('Si quieren Frontear'), and Bad Gyal ('Real G'), among many others. These projects have propelled Spanish reggaeton onto the world stage, consolidating Quevedo as one of the main figures in the urban music scene and maintaining his undeniable market leadership.
